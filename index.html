Why Sales?

My interest in sales began when I worked as a Vacation Planner at Universal Orlando Resort. Working in vacation planning takes an enthusiastic and hardworking individual as it is your job to address guests, resolve problems, and help them achieve their best Universal experience. As a Vacation Planner, it is our job to figure out the wants and needs of guests and then present them with the options that will fulfill as many of them, if not all, as possible.

I always loved being the one to help guests plan their vacations. The process of planning vacations, or even short visits, can be stressful for guests as they are trying to ensure they get the option that best fits them, their situation, and their wants and needs. Being able to relieve that stress and walk them through the different aspects of their stay from hotels, theme park tickets, and dining options is always something I enjoyed. In the process of helping guests plan their vacations, I would often build relationships, explain different vacation options, and discuss and negotiate prices. While I loved my job, what I loved was the sales process.

This job helped me realize my passion for sales. It helped me to realize that this is something I want to pursue as a career. And it is ultimately what made me interested in the Professional Selling Program.

The Professional Selling Program

The Professional Selling Program is a prestigious and highly selective program offered by the Marketing Department of the UCF College of Business Administration. The program accepts no more than 35 students per year, and is designed to develop in them the skills and abilities necessary to become future sales leaders.

This comprehensive program uses its extensive resources and hands-on learning methods to prepare students for sales in thriving industries such as technology, consumer goods, advertising, pharmaceuticals, and more.

The Professional Selling program is preparing its participants for today's fierce job market through rigorous training and mentorship. I am honored to have been selected for this exceptional program.

Coursework

MAR 3391 - Professional Selling


    Core Concepts:

    The full sales process, including proper use of sales techniques, practices​ and terminology.
    Demonstrate professional written and oral communication related to sales and personal branding, including a portfolio and role plays.
    Demonstrate critical thinking skills by analyzing customer problems, applying proper logic, and formulating recommendations based on their analyses.

    Assignments:

    A speed test designed to test the ability of the student to relay important information about themselves and why they would fit into a position or company in a short amount of time.
    Sales role plays designed to test the students ability to apply conceptual knowledge of the sales process based on real-life scenarios.
    A series of three real-world role play scenarios that test the application of conceptual knowledge of the sales process.

MAR 3203 - Supply Chain and Operations Management

    Core Concepts:

    Understand the tradeoffs among different types of supply chain and operation systems in terms of key characteristics, management tasks, organization control, and impact on the strategy and direction of the firm.
    Develop a basic competence with the tools and techniques used by supply chain and operations professionals in setting and executing firm policies.
    Develop an understanding of the managerial implications of alternative supply chain and operations policies and techniques.

    Assignments:

    Simulations designed to recreate real-world supply chain situations where students are able to see the effects of decisions and apply their conceptual knowledge.
    Video case study assignments in which students are able to see how class concepts are applied in real businesses.

MAR 3721 - Digital Media Marketing


    Core Concepts:

    Website design to help improve conversion rates and search engine optimization.
    Best practices for digital media ad campaigns (search engine marketing, display ads, email marketing, and social media marketing).

    Assignments:

    Simulations which allow students to create their own advertisements, evaluate their effectiveness, and make changes to improve them based on analytics.
    Develop a landing page for an assigned fictional company, apply conceptual knowledge, and view the effectiveness of your decision through simulated analytics.
    Collaborate with an Orlando marketing agency (South Street and Co.) to develop a social media campaign for their Around Winterpark account.

MAR 3503 - Consumer Behavior


    Core Concepts:

    Understand the thought processes of consumers and the psychology of consumption.
    Understand the importance of and how to improve the consumer experience.
    Develop a set of skills important to successful performance in managerial positions such as application of class material, group work, written skills, and presentation skills.

    Assignments:

    Consumer Behavior Audit project in which a group will analyze a brands advertising strategy, positioning, and marketing.
    Consumer Behavior Audit presentation in which a group will present the marketing, advertising and positioning efforts of the brand and any recommendation they have which could help the brand to better reach their target audience.
    In-class case study discussions in which groups are randomly assigned and students discuss the strategies of an assigned brand and their effectiveness to be presented to the class.

Philanthropy

Community service and philanthropy have always been passions of mine. It is for this reason that in Fall of 2019 I joined the Alpha Xi chapter of Omega Phi Alpha National Service Sorority. Omega Phi Alpha is a service based organization that focuses both on individual community service and philanthropy, as well as group service. Our group service benefits the university community, the community at large, the members of the sorority, the nations of the world, and one more cause which changes bi-annually.

Some of my favorite community service events we have done are Feeding Children Everywhere, and raising money to benefit the LoveLand Therapy Fund. Feeding Children Everywhere is a non-profit organization committed to providing healthy meals to those in need. They utilize volunteers to package meals and have them sent world-wide to those facing food insecurity. The LoveLand Therapy Fund is an organization that provides free therapy sessions to black women and girls in need.
